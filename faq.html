The frequency of filter changes depends on several factors, including:

  • Water quality: Poor quality water requires more frequent changes
  • System type: Different systems have different recommendations
  • Usage: Higher water consumption means filters need changing sooner
  • Filter type: Sediment filters typically last 6-12 months, while carbon filters last 12-24 months

As a general guideline, we recommend changing filters every 6 to 12 months. We provide filter replacement kits and can help you determine the best schedule for your specific system.

Reverse osmosis (RO) is a water purification technology that uses a semipermeable membrane to remove ions, unwanted molecules, and larger particles from water.

Here's how it works:

  • Water is forced through a membrane with microscopic pores (0.0001 microns)
  • Contaminants larger than water molecules are filtered out
  • The result is ultra-pure water with up to 99% of impurities removed

Benefits:

  • Removes bacteria, viruses, and dissolved minerals
  • Improves taste and odor of water
  • Reduces salt, fluoride, and other harmful substances
  • Cost-effective compared to bottled water

Proper maintenance ensures your system runs efficiently and provides clean water. Follow these guidelines:

Regular Maintenance Tasks:

  • Monthly: Clean the exterior and check for leaks
  • Every 3 months: Check filter condition and water quality
  • Every 6-12 months: Replace filters according to manufacturer schedule
  • Annually: Professional inspection and system flush

Step-by-Step Maintenance Guide:

  • Turn off the main water supply before any maintenance
  • Release pressure from the system if applicable
  • Follow the filter replacement procedure in your manual
  • Flush the system after filter replacement
  • Keep a maintenance log with dates and changes
  • Store replacement filters in a cool, dry place

Professional Maintenance:

  • We offer annual maintenance packages
  • Includes inspection, cleaning, and minor repairs
  • Warranty customers receive discounted maintenance
